Chapter 5: DACs and Streamers — Pre-Quiz One-Pager

1. What is a DAC? (Digital-to-Analog Converter)

A DAC converts digital audio signals (like those from streaming services, CDs, or your phone) into analog signals that your amplifier and speakers can play.

Without a good DAC, your system won’t deliver high-quality sound — even with great speakers and amps.

Built-in vs. External DACs: Many devices (AVRs, smartphones) have built-in DACs, but external DACs often offer better sound quality by reducing noise and improving detail.

2. Why does a good DAC matter?

  • Clarity and Detail: Higher-quality DACs offer clearer sound with more detail.
  • Dynamic Range: A good DAC preserves quiet and loud sounds without distortion.
  • Low Jitter: Reduces timing errors in digital audio playback, keeping the music smooth and accurate.

3. What’s a Streamer?

A streamer is a device that connects to online music services (like Spotify, Tidal, or Apple Music) and sends digital audio signals to your DAC or amplifier.

Streamers often support hi-res audio formats like FLAC, WAV, or MQA for better sound quality than Bluetooth or MP3.

4. Why not just use Bluetooth?

  • Sound Quality: Bluetooth compresses audio, reducing detail. Streamers often use Wi-Fi or Ethernet for lossless, higher-quality audio.
  • Stability: Streamers offer more stable connections and support higher bitrates and sample rates.
