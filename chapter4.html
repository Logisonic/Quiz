Chapter 4: What is an Amplifier/AVR?

An amplifier (amp) boosts a weak audio signal to a level strong enough to drive speakers. An AVR (Audio/Video Receiver) is an amplifier designed for home theater, with extra features like surround sound decoding and multiple inputs.

1. Amplifier Components

  • Power Supply: Converts AC electricity into DC power for amplification.
  • Preamp: Processes and controls input signals (volume, EQ, source selection).
  • Power Amp: Amplifies the signal to drive speakers.
  • Speaker Terminals: Connects the amp to speakers (binding posts or banana plugs).

2. Key Amplifier Properties

  • Wattage (Power Output, W RMS): How much power the amp delivers continuously per channel.
    Example: 50W RMS per channel into 8Ω means each channel delivers 50W at a standard impedance.
  • Total Harmonic Distortion (THD%): Measures signal purity. Lower is better (ideally below 0.1%).
  • Damping Factor: The amp’s ability to control speaker movement, affecting bass tightness. Higher is better.
  • Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR, dB): How much louder the signal is compared to noise. Higher is better (above 90 dB).

3. Amplifier Classes

Amplifiers operate in different classes, affecting efficiency and sound quality:

  • Class A: Purest sound but inefficient and hot.
  • Class AB: A balance of quality and efficiency (common in HiFi).
  • Class D: Very efficient, smaller, and cooler (common in modern AVRs and subwoofers).

4. AVR Features for Home Theater

  • Channels: Determines speaker configuration (e.g., 5.1, 7.1, 9.2).
  • Dolby/DTS Decoding: Supports surround sound formats like Dolby Atmos, DTS:X.
  • Room Calibration: (e.g., Audyssey, YPAO, Dirac Live) Automatically adjusts settings for better sound.
  • HDMI & Connectivity: Supports 4K, HDR, ARC, eARC for modern home theater setups.

5. Matching Amplifiers and Speakers

An amp must be matched properly to the speakers for optimal performance—later chapters will cover impedance matching, power requirements, and amplifier/speaker compatibility in more detail!
